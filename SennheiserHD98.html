For the times when you want to hear the space and depth of recordings and films, but must wear headphones, the Sennheiser HD 598 creates a sonic field that seems lifelike. At $249.95, these are headphones for the home theater or hi-fi stereo fanatic who can't always listen through a surround sound system or top-notch speakers. The best part is the excellent spatial depth the HD 598 provides is 100-percent acoustic design, sans digital effects or attempts to mimic surround sound. The HD 598 is headphone home audio at its best, easily earning our Editors' Choice award. The circumaural (over-the-ear) headphones are intended for home use, which will make the brown-and-cream color scheme less of an issue if you find it unappealing. We like the retro styling, complete with velour earpads, but it's not for everyone. The included removable audio cable has two characteristics uncommon to most headphones available today: it terminates in a 1/4-inch connection (though a 3.5mm adapter is included for use with mobile devices and computers with smaller headphone jacks), and it lacks a remote control or microphone. The headphones are exceedingly comfortable over long listening periods. In other words, Sennheiser designed the HD 598 to help you get lost in your music or movies, without communication or mobile features. On tracks with powerful sub-bass content, like The Knife's "Silent Shout," the HD 598 delivers an intense, thumping low-frequency response matched by a near-perfect representation of the high-mids and highs. This sound is the very definition of balance, with rich lows and absolutely no distortion even at top volumes. A track like this, with its panned synth portions, gives us a decent example of the sense of space the open-back design provides, but tracks that have more of a sense of space in their mixes truly allow the HD 598 to shine.
